‚≠ê Featured Stories of Censorship

The Satanic Verses

Banned in 14 countries

Salman Rushdie's magical realist novel sparked international controversy in 1988. The book's portrayal of Islamic themes led to death threats, a fatwa calling for Rushdie's assassination, and bans across the Muslim world. Rushdie lived in hiding for years, yet the book became a symbol of artistic freedom.

Animal Farm

Banned in 6 countries

George Orwell's political allegory was banned by both communist and fascist regimes who recognized themselves in its critique of totalitarianism. The story of farm animals overthrowing their human master became too dangerous a metaphor for revolutions that had gone wrong.

Ulysses

Banned in 2 countries

James Joyce's modernist epic faced bans and seizures for its candid portrayal of sexuality and its experimental style. The 1933 U.S. court decision clearing Ulysses became a landmark victory for literary freedom and helped reshape censorship standards.
